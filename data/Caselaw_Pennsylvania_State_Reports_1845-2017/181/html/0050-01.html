The Pennsylvania Company for Insurance on Lives and Granting Annuities, Trustee and Administrator of C. H. Baker, deceased, v. The American Fire Insurance Company, Appellant.

Argued March 22, 1897.

Appeal, No. 437, Jan. T., 1896, by defendant, from decree of C. P. No. 4, Phila. Co., June T., 1893, No. 770, on bill in equity.

Before Geeen, Williams, Mitchell, Dean and Fell, JJ.

Affirmed.

JS. Sunn Sanson with him J. P. Townsend, for appellant.

John G. Johnson and Geo. Tucker Bispham, with them John Sampton Barnes, for appellee.

April 19, 1897 :

Opinion by

Me. Justice Dean,

There is no substantial distinction between the facts in this case and those in the case between same plaintiff and Franklin Fire Insurance Company, in which last, opinion and decree have been handed down this day, ante, p. 40. For the reasons given in that case, the decree of the court below is affirmed in this one.
