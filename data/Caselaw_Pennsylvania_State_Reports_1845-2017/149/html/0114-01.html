Duncan, et al., Appellants, v. Hartman.

Heal estate—Agent—Power to “manage ”—Sight to make exclusive grant to quarry—Informal execution.

Duncan v. Hartman, 113 Pa. 595, affirmed.

Argued April 19, 1892.

Appeal, No. 809, Jan. T., 1892, by plaintiffs, Peter S. Duncan et al., from judgment of C. P. Blair Co., Oct. T., 1888, No. 58, on verdict for defendant, Jesse L. Hartman.

Before Pans on, C. J., Green, Williams, Mitchell and Heydrick, JJ.

M. M. Baldridge and Alexander King, for appellants.

Martin Bell, John B. Blair and K. P. Mervine with him, for appellee.

May 2, 1892:

Per Curiam,

This case was here upon a former appeal, and is reported in 148 Pa. 595. As the learned judge below has followed our rulings in that case, it would be unprofitable to discuss it further.

Judgment affirmed.
