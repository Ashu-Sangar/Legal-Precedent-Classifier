Cope’s Estate.

Argued November 26,1937.

Before Schaffer, Maxey, Drew, Linn, Stern and Barnes, JJ.

Henry D. O’Connor, with Mm A. Archer Cross, for appellant.

Robert G. ErsMne, with him Townsend, Elliott & Munson and George Ross, for appellees, were not heard.

January 3, 1938:

Per Curiam,

This is an appeal from the decree of the orphans’ court dismissing a petition for review of an adjudication entered June 2, 1898. Over thirty-nine years having elapsed since the adjudication was entered, it is now too late to reconsider the questions there determined, in the absence of any showing of actual fraud: Elkins’s Est., 325 Pa. 373, 190 A. 650; Knox’s Est. (No. 1), 328 Pa. 177, 195 A. 28. The situation is not altered by the *297petitioner’s disposition to call an alleged error on the part of the auditing judge a constructive fraud.

Appeal dismissed at appellant’s cost.
