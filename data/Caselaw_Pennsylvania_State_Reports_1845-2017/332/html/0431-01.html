Salus et al., Appellants, v. Lawrence, Secretary of Commonwealth et al.

Argued December 28, 1938.

Before Kephart, C. J., Schaffer, Linn, Stern and Barnes, JJ.

*432 Marshall H. Morgan, with him Earl V. Compton, for appellants.

Lemuel B. Schofield and J ames Todaro, Deputy Attorney General, with them Cuy K. Bard, Attorney General, and J. Dress Pannell, for appellees.

December 31, 1938:

Per Curiam,

The five Judges who heard this appeal all agree that it must be dismissed. We have held in a number of cases that: “While the granting or refusal of a preliminary injunction is the subject of appeal, yet in such case we refrain from a discussion of the merits of the litigation and merely determine whether, under the facts presented in the court below, there was a reasonable ground for its action”: Holden v. Llewellyn et al., 262 Pa. 400, at pp. 402 and 403.*

The questions involved come from the Election Code of June 3, 1937, P. L. 1333, and we find that the court below had “reasonable ground for its action.”

Appeal dismissed.
