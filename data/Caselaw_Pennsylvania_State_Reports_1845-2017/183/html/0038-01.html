Commonwealth v. B. F. W. Urban, late Clerk of Oyer and Terminer and Clerk of Quarter Sessions of Lancaster, Appellant.

Argued May 81, 1897.

Appeal, No. 2, May T., 1897, by defendant, from judgment of C. P. Dauphin Co., Jan. T., 1895, No. 494, on appeal from settlement of auditor general.

Before Sterrett, C. J., Green, Williams, McCollum, Mitchell, Dean and Fell, JJ.

Affirmed.

IT. M. North, with him J. Ray Brown, and J. W. Benlinger, for appellant.

Ron. J. P. Elkin, deputy attorney general, with him Ron. R. O. MoGormiok, attorney general, for appellee.

October 11, 1897:

Opinion by

Mr. Justice Green,

For the reasons stated in the opinion just filed in the case of Commonwealth v. Fry, No. 1, May term, 1897, ante, p. 32, the judgment in this case is affirmed.

Judgment affirmed and appeal dismissed at the cost of the appellant.
