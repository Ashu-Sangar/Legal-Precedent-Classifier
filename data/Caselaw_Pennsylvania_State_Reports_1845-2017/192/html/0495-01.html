Commonwealth ex rel. W. U. Hensel, Attorney General, v. Order of Solon. Appeal of James Fitzsimmons.

Argued May 80, 1899.

Appeal, No. 21, May T., 1899, by James Fitzsimmons, from order of C. P. Dauphin Co., June T., 1894, No. 310, dismissing exceptions to auditors’ report.

Before Sterrett, C. J., Green, Mitchell, Dean and Fell, JJ.

Affirmed.

O. A. O'Brien, for appellant.

Lyman JD. GHlbert, with him Josiah H. Quincy, for appellee.

July 19, 1899:

Opinion by

Mb. Chief Justice Steerett,

This case was heard and considered with two other cases involving substantially the same questions. In one of these, Commonwealth ex rel. Attorney General v. The Order of Solon, L. K. Porter’s Appeal, No. 18, May term, 1899, an opinion has just been filed affirming the decree, etc., ante, p. 487. For reasons therein given, the decree in this case is also affirmed and the appeal dismissed at appellant’s costs.
