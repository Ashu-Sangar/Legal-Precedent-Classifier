Dziengielewski, Appellant, v. Dickson City School District.

*25Argued January 23, 1934.

Before Frazer, C. J., Simpson, Kephakt, Schaffer, Maxey, Drew and Linn, JJ.

Clarence J. Wing, with him Frederick E. Scott and James C. McDonough, for appellant.

Walter W. Harris of O’Malley, Hill, Harris & Harris, with him Leo C. Knoll, for appellee.

January 30, 1934:

Per Curiam,

In this case the final judgment of the court below was entered of record on June 28, 1933. The appeal affidavit was filed with the prothonotary of the Supreme Court on September 16, 1933, which was within the three months prescribed by the Act of May 19, 1897, P. L. 67, as amended, but the writ of certiorari was not lodged with the prothonotary of the lower court, nor was notice given until November 25, 1933. The appeal was consequently not perfected until nearly two months after expiration of the statutory period. Appellant has offered no satisfactory reason in explanation of this delay and we are of opinion the appeal should be quashed for the reason the writ of certiorari was not filed within the prescribed three months or a reasonable time thereafter. See Donley v. Semans, 260 Pa. 88, and Real Estate & *26Mtg. Company v. Duquesne Light Co., 99 Pa. Superior Ct. 222.

Appeal quashed at appellant’s costs.
