Stamey, Appellant, v. Dunlap.

Argued Oct. 18, 1904.

Appeal, No. 21, Oct. T., 1904, by plaintiffs, from judgment of C. P. Indiana Co., June T., 1903, No. 143, on verdict for defendant in issue framed between W. H. Stamey, J. C. King and E. D. Carter as plaintiffs and John Dunlap as defendant.

Before Mitchell, C. J., Dean, Fell, Beown, Mesteezat, Pottee and Elkin, JJ.

Affirmed.

April 10, 1905:

Opinion by

Mr. Justice Beown,

The questions raised by the assignments of error in this case are the same as those raised in Stamey et al. v. Barkley, 211 *647Pa. 313, in which we have this day filed an opinion affirming the judgment below, and, for the reasons therein given, this judgment is affirmed.
