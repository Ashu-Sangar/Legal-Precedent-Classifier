Kline et al., Admrs., v. Kline et al., Appellants.

Argued October 13, 1936.

Before Kephart, C. J., Schaffer, Drew, Linn, Stern and Barnes, JJ.

Geo. E. Whitmer, with him A. A. Geary, for appellant.

Theo. L. Wilson, with him John M. Myers, for appellees.

November 23, 1936:

Per Curiam,

This appeal is from an order setting aside a sheriff’s' sale. The condition upon which the order was made re*146quired the purchaser to bid the amount at which the property was sold to him provided the holder of the first mortgage agreed to satisfy it and, if not, the bid should be the original sum less the amount of the first mortgage. When a sheriff’s sale is set aside by a court, the order will not be disturbed on appeal unless there is manifest or gross abuse of discretion: Lefever v. Kline, 294 Pa. 22, 25; Beaver County B. & L. Association v. Winowich, 323 Pa. 483.

It is clear from a careful review of the evidence that the bidders were under a misapprehension as to the effect of the sale on liens of record. The court below did not abuse its discretion in setting aside the sale and attaching thereto the conditions stated.

The decree of the court below is affirmed at appellants’ cost.
